# Demographic Structure Analysis Report

## Project

Albania’s Brain Drain: A Data-Driven Analysis of Migration, Employment and Economic Development

## Purpose

This report summarizes Albania's demographic structure using the World Bank Population Estimates dataset.

The analysis focuses on youth population, working-age population, elderly population, dependency pressure, and projections to 2035.

## Key Demographic Indicators

- Population in 2000: 3,089,027
- Latest available population year: 2025
- Latest available population: 2,355,297
- Projected population in 2035: 2,155,920

## Population Change

- Population change from 2000 to latest available year: -23.75%
- Population change from 2000 to 2035 projection: -30.21%

## Working-Age Population

- Working-age population in 2000: 1,947,712
- Latest working-age population: 1,549,119
- Projected working-age population in 2035: 1,362,789
- Working-age population change from 2000 to latest year: -20.46%
- Working-age population change from 2000 to 2035 projection: -30.03%

## Elderly Population

- Elderly population in 2000: 226,447
- Latest elderly population: 415,053
- Projected elderly population in 2035: 506,156
- Elderly population change from 2000 to latest year: 83.29%
- Elderly population change from 2000 to 2035 projection: 123.52%

## Interpretation

The demographic structure analysis helps explain why brain drain matters beyond migration numbers.

If the working-age population declines while the elderly population increases, Albania may face stronger pressure on the labour market, pension system, healthcare system, and long-term economic growth.

Youth and working-age migration can reduce the future labour force and create skill shortages, especially if educated or economically active people leave the country.

## Limitations

- The projection values are based on population estimates and should not be interpreted as guaranteed future outcomes.
- Population structure can change due to migration policy, fertility, mortality, economic conditions, and return migration.
- Age-group indicators are useful for demographic risk analysis, but they do not directly identify education level or skill level of migrants.
